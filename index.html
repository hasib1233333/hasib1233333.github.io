§ 2.1 — Profile
Md Hasibuzzaman

I am a graduate researcher and Ph.D aspirant at Asia University, Taiwan, pursuing my M.E. in Computer Science and Information Engineering with a perfect GPA of 4.30/4.30. My research is conducted at the Big Data Lab, focused on robotics, dynamic path planning, and image processing for autonomous mobile robots.

My core thesis investigates skeletonization-based on object wave propagation for mobile robot navigation in unknown environments — developing novel collision avoidance algorithms that combine Euclidean distance transforms with topological skeleton extraction (EGF four-stage method).

I am a recipient of the prestigious Taiwan Ministry of Education (MOE) Scholarship 2024, recognizing academic excellence in my graduate program. Prior to graduate studies, I completed B.Tech in Electrical & Electronics Engineering at Lovely Professional University, India (GPA 8.18/10) with multiple international exchange programs.

🎯 Path Planning Strategy (Based on Research Image)

Step 1: Source to Skeleton

Find the nearest skeleton point from the source using Euclidean distance. Connect source to this skeleton entry point with a straight line (if clear) or 8-connected Grid-based Wavefront Algorithm.

Step 2: Skeleton Navigation

Follow the skeleton path from entry point to exit point. The skeleton provides maximum clearance from obstacles, ensuring safe navigation through the environment.

Step 3: Skeleton to Destination

Find the nearest skeleton point to the destination. Connect this skeleton exit point to the destination with a straight line (if clear) or 8-connected Grid-based Wavefront Algorithm.

Advantage: This method combines the safety of skeleton-based navigation (maximum clearance from obstacles) with the efficiency of direct connections at endpoints, resulting in smooth, collision-free paths.

Biography

Gene Eu Jan received the B.S. degree in electrical engineering from National Taiwan University, Taipei, Taiwan, in 1982 and the M.S. and Ph.D. degrees in electrical and computer engineering from the University of Maryland, College Park, MD, USA, in 1988 and 1992, respectively.

He has been a Professor with the Departments of Computer Science and Electrical Engineering, National Taipei University, New Taipei City, Taiwan, since 2004, where he was also the Dean of the College of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from 2007 to 2009. In addition, Professor Jan served as the President of Tainan National University of the Arts from August 2016 to July 2024.

Since 2024, he serves as a Chair Professor with the Department of Computer Science, Asia University, Taiwan. He has published more than 270 articles related to data science, parallel computer systems, interconnection networks, motion planning, electronic design automation, and VLSI systems design in journals, conference proceedings, books and patents.
